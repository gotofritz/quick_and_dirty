Watched: Back to Future

I managed to watch these with the kids just before I tested positive. If there is one 80s classic one has cannot miss this is it. Because of its influence on every time travel plot that came after. I was expecting the worst, but Zemeckis & Co. surprised me. Cheesy, of course, but they handle the time travel paradoxes well. Better than The Flash, which came out 4 decades later. The over acting is irritating, particularly Fox, but the dystopian scenarios quite good. The kids loved it too.
I am sure I am not the first to notice that Biff's dystopian world == Trump's America, right? And I can only image what the woke brigade would have made of the movie had it came out today. With McFly inventing rock'n'roll, and teaching it to a black man. I quite liked how thy alluded to the whole Civil Rights movement without needing to get into it. The car from the future running on recycled rubbish was also a good touch.

Cooked: focaccia

I used to make my own focaccia every week when I lived in London. I had my own sourdough starter and everything. But I stopped when I moved to Germany. No need to, the bread here is excellent. In England they sell this super-light spongy material which they call "sliced bread". It has the same nutritional value as shoelaces, but none of their tastiness.
Just before covid hit us I had bought a cube of fresh yeast on a whim. The perfect activity to keep us entertained in those quarantine days! Actually, focaccia is quick to make, the entertainment didn't last that long. The baking tray I used was too small; it rose quite a lot in the middle, and the end result was underwhelming. Next time.

Cooked: pho

Wife craved Asian noodle soup in the early, fluey stages of her covid. I had bookmarked a vegan pho recipe on Bon Appetit only a day earlier. I do very little vegan stock these days. Making a tasty stock without meat is an art which requires effort. More effort than I am prepared to put in for the little amounts I use. Freezing doesn't help, defrosted veg stock is just dirty water. But it was for a good cause, so I cooked the pho. To my surprise, not only was it super easy, barely an inconvenience, but very tasty indeed. Will do again.

Watching: The Wire

HBO wasn't a thing in the UK when The Wire was out, so we never got into it. Luckily I had just been to the library the Saturday before the infection struck. I had gotten the whole first series out on DVD. The perfect entertainment for the quarantine! I have no deep insights on the series, it's brilliant, we are loving it it. I'll hunt down all the other series when I get out.
